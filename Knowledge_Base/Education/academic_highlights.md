## Senior Design Project (Capstone)

### Bone Conduction Railing System

**Duration:** September 2022 – April 2023 (Two-semester capstone project)

**Sponsor:** University of Toledo College of Arts and Letters, UT Green Fund

**Team:** Multidisciplinary team (EECS and Mechanical Engineering students)

#### Project overview

Created an interactive art installation to commemorate the 150th Anniversary of the University of Toledo. The system uses bone conduction technology to transmit historical audio clips through a railing, allowing users to hear campus sounds (athletic games, arts performances, commencement speeches) by placing their elbows on the railing—without any audible leakage to the surrounding environment.

**Inspiration:** The "Touched Echo" art installation in Dresden, Germany, which uses bone conduction to share historical sounds of WWII bombing.

#### My role & contributions

As part of the EECS team, I focused on the **software and audio systems**:

- **Arduino Programming (C++):** Developed software to play audio files in an infinite loop from an SD card, ensuring continuous operation with minimal maintenance.
- **Audio Pipeline:** Converted audio files to WAV format and integrated them with Arduino libraries to achieve high-quality playback.
- **System Integration:** Coordinated with the amplifier, filter system, and transducer hardware to ensure seamless audio transmission through the railing.
- **Testing & Optimization:** Conducted unit testing on the Arduino system, speaker system, and amplifier to ensure functionality before final integration.

#### Technical specifications

- **Microcontroller:** Arduino Duo with Arduino Shield
- **Audio Source:** SD card with WAV files
- **Amplifier & Transducers:** Two separate speakers (one for frequencies ≤100 Hz, one for >100 Hz)
- **Materials:** Aluminum railing (final design) for optimal audio conduction with minimal leakage
- **Power:** Standard electrical outlet

#### Design & testing process

1. **Collaboration Phase (Fall 2022):** Worked with Mechanical Engineering students who designed and built the physical railing prototype. Our EECS team researched speaker, amplifier, and software requirements.
2. **Software Development:** Implemented audio playback software on Arduino, debugged playback speed issues, and added loop functionality.
3. **Audio Quality Testing:** Used Room Enhancement Wizard software and specialized microphones to measure:
   - **Conduction Intensity:** Sound transmitted through the railing
   - **Leakage Intensity:** Sound escaping into the environment
4. **Iteration:** Tested multiple railing materials (steel vs. aluminum) and speaker configurations. Final design achieved a 10 dB improvement in the conduction-to-leakage ratio.

#### Key outcomes

- Successfully delivered a working prototype installed indoors with a sleek housing enclosure for all hardware components.
- Achieved design goals: audible to users in contact, inaudible to non-users, minimal maintenance, and safe operation.
- Applied industry standards: IEC 60268-3 (amplifiers) and ANSI S3.13 (bone conduction devices).
- Gained hands-on experience with cross-disciplinary collaboration, real-world client requirements, regulatory standards, and full-system testing.

#### Skills demonstrated

- Embedded systems programming (Arduino, C++)
- Audio signal processing and system integration
- Cross-functional teamwork (EECS + Mechanical Engineering)
- Requirements gathering, testing methodologies, and quality assurance
- Patent research and ethical considerations (bone conduction technology IP)
