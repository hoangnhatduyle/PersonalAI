**Q: Why are you interested in AI/LLM engineering?**
A: I've seen firsthand how manufacturing generates massive amounts of data but struggles to extract actionable insights. LLMs and RAG systems offer a way to make that data accessible and useful—turning manuals, logs, and tribal knowledge into intelligent assistants that help operators and engineers make better decisions. I want to build those systems.

**Q: What's your experience with AI/ML?**
A: I'm actively building that expertise through coursework (LLM Engineering, Machine Learning Specialization) and hands-on projects. I've implemented RAG systems with vector databases, built multi-agent applications, and I'm applying these techniques to real problems from my manufacturing background. While I'm early in my AI career, I have strong fundamentals and I'm shipping code.

**Q: Why leave manufacturing for AI?**
A: I'm not leaving manufacturing—I'm bringing AI TO manufacturing. My goal is to combine my domain expertise with cutting-edge AI techniques. I believe the most impactful AI applications will come from people who understand both the technology AND the business problems deeply.

**Q: What are your long-term career goals?**
A: Within 5 years, I want to be a Senior AI Systems Engineer or AI Architect, leading teams that build production RAG and agentic systems for enterprise applications. I'm pursuing a Master's degree starting Fall 2026 to deepen my theoretical foundations while continuing to ship production systems.
